ICT for Social Change: An Interactive Guide

ICT as a Platform for Change

Information and Communication Technology (ICT) is a powerful medium for advocacy and driving social change. It leverages online platforms to promote causes, disseminate information, and mobilize communities. This section explores the theories behind technology's role in societal shifts, historical examples, and the profound impact of social media.

Ogburn's Theory on Technology & Social Change

William Fielding Ogburn proposed that technology is the primary driver of social change, highlighting three stages of technical development:

1. Invention

New forms of technology are developed. E.g., the computer and communication tech allowed rapid info spread and social networking.

2. Discovery

New ways of understanding reality. E.g., computer tech changed how people perceive and acquire info about distant events.

3. Diffusion

The spread of technology to other areas. E.g., computer tech spread from America/Europe to Asia/Africa, with cultural adjustments.

ICT in Philippine History

ICT has played a pivotal role in several key historical moments in the Philippines:

EDSA People Power (1986)

Cardinal Sin's call to action via radio broadcast (Radio Veritas) mobilized citizens.

EDSA II (2001)

Text messaging was used to mobilize people against President Estrada.

PhilAWARE Application

Aims to increase disaster resilience through crowd-sourced reporting and government validation.

The Social Power of Social Media

Social media is a prime tool for spreading advocacies. Explore some impactful movements:

Hong Kong Protests (2019)

Used for live news and action info against an extradition bill.

ALS Ice Bucket Challenge (2014)

Raised money and awareness for ALS via a viral nomination challenge.

Black Lives Matter (2013+)

Online community using #BlackLivesMatter against anti-black racism.

Me Too Movement (2017+)

Solidarity for sexual harassment survivors using #MeToo.

Million People March (PH, 2013)

Protest against Pork Barrel fund scam, organized via social media.

How Social Media Promotes Social Justice:

  • Coordinating Community Responses:
    Creates online spaces for support and idea-sharing.
  • Platforming Lived Experience:
    Amplifies stories of minorities and underrepresented groups.
  • Sharing Pictures and Videos:
    Documents events and redirects narratives.

Related Terms:

Slacktivism

Simple online measures to support a social movement (e.g., signing petitions, reposting statuses/images).

Cancel Culture

Withdrawing support for public figures/companies over controversial acts, often a form of online shaming.

Crafting Your ICT Project for Social Change

Successfully developing an ICT project for social change requires careful planning, conceptualization, and understanding your audience. This section delves into defining project scope, gathering requirements, profiling your target audience, and the essentials of project construction and reporting.

Planning & Conceptualizing

A Project Scope Statement is crucial. Its elements include:

States the overview of the project concisely.
Key features/outcomes (e.g., budget report, design drawing).
Specific conditions for completion (e.g., 50 views, 15 shares).
Limitations/risks (time, money, scope - the triple constraints).
What is NOT included in the project.

Techniques for Gathering Requirements:

Brainstorming
Group idea generation and filtering.
Nominal Group Technique
Structured: problem intro -> silent idea generation -> sharing -> prioritization.
Delphi Technique
Collects expert opinions via multiple question rounds, refining to consensus.

Audience Profiling

Understanding your audience is key. This involves acquiring data and segmenting it.

Four Stages of Audience Profiling:

Segmentation
Dividing audience by demographics, psychographics etc.
Messaging
Developing tailored messages for segments.
Engagement
Understanding when/where engagement drops.
Measurement
Quantifying reach to fine-tune approach.

Steps for Target Audience Profile:

  • Collecting Data (surveys for demographics)
  • Analyzing Psychographics (interests, attitudes)
  • Combining Data (for a targeted approach)

Constructing the ICT Project: Progress Report

A progress report is vital for tracking and communication. Consider these factors:

1. Tone

Clear, concise, honest. Avoid defensive language. Explain mistakes.

2. Format

Consistent template, headings (Project Info, Work Completed, Schedule).

3. Content

  • Subject Line: Name & Date.
  • Introduction: Title, date, participants, status.
  • Body: Costs, work completed, schedule, complications.
  • Conclusion: Summary, delays, recommendations.

Launching & Growing Your ICT Project

Publishing your ICT project is a significant milestone. To ensure its success and sustainability, understanding content marketing, traffic monitoring, and how to effectively use feedback is crucial. This section covers these key aspects to help your project reach its intended audience and thrive.

Content Marketing & SEO

A strategic approach to create and distribute valuable content.

Advertising Effects:

Awareness
Increased visibility on platforms. Stick to core message.
Loyalty
Builds audience trust and repeat interaction.
Authority
Become a leader in shaping narratives. Post accurate content.

SEO & Keyword Optimization:

  • Focus on 1-2 keywords (avoid stuffing).
  • Use keywords in the title (question-based ideal).
  • Use long-tail keywords naturally.

Traffic Monitoring

Tracking content engagement is vital.

Key Metrics:

Unique Pageviews
Number of distinct visitors.
Traffic Sources
Where traffic comes from (direct, referral, search).
Social Media Shares
More shares = wider organic reach.
Other Social Engagements
Reactions, likes, retweets, upvotes provide direct feedback.

Content Strategy to Boost:

Paid Advertising Brand Collaboration

Feedback and Interaction

Soliciting and using feedback optimizes content.

Key Figures for Feedback:

Ratings

Using a defined scale (e.g., 5-star).

Acknowledgments

Likes, favorites, upvotes, virtual gifts.

Comments

Text feedback; can spark discussion, may need moderation.

Ways to Use Feedback in Content Marketing:

Personalized responses, makes audience feel seen.
Shows appreciation, serves as testimonials.
Personalized responses enhance connection.
Use negative feedback to refine strategies (collaborators, imagery, ad exposure).
